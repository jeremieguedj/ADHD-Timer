## ADHD & Kids UX Principles

- **Visual countdown over digits** — pie-wedge timer (like Time Timer) gives kids a spatial sense of "how much is left" without needing to read numbers
- **Graduated warnings, never sudden cutoffs** — 5 escalating reminders (50%, 25%, 10min, 5min, 90sec) prepare the brain for transition instead of triggering a meltdown
- **Gradual dopamine ramp-down** — periodic reminders gently pull the child out of hyperfocus in stages, avoiding the crash of going from full immersion to sudden stop
- **Pause-and-acknowledge** — video pauses during reminders, forcing a brief attention shift that helps break hyperfocus
- **Positive reinforcement at the end** — celebration page with stars and a waving bunny reframes "screen time is over" as an achievement, not a punishment
- **Friendly character companion** — the bunny is the messenger, not the parent — reduces oppositional resistance
- **Sensory-gentle design** — no harsh colors, no loud sounds, respects `prefers-reduced-motion`, timer grows gradually rather than flashing
- **No reading required** — color-coded pie, animated bunny, spoken reminders — works for pre-readers and kids who struggle with text processing under stress
